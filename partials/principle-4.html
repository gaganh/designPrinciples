Things should be designed to be useable, without modification, by as many people as possible.

  • Historically, accessibility in design focused on accomodating people with disabilities.
  • Modern practice accepts that most accomodations can be designed to benifit everyone.,
  • Make things perceptible by everyone. For example, position controls and information so that seated and standing people can percieve them.
  • Make things learnable by everyone. for example, use redundant coding methods (e.g., textual, iconic, and tactile) on controls and signage.
  • Make the consequences of errors non-catastrophic, and reversible whenever possible.
