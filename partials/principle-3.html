Measure things as close to their action as possible.

  • Proposed by Geramn physicist Ernest Abbe, these are angular errors that increase with distance.
  • The best measurements are achieve when taken as close eto the action as possible. For example, placing a temperature sensor near a heat source will result in more accurate measurements than a sensor placed farther away.
  • The principle also has implications for the design of mechanisms. Actuators and bearings should be placed as close to lines of action as possible.
